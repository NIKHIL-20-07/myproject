WHO's International Travel and Health guidance:

  • Pre-Travel Readiness:Seek advice at least 4 to 8 weeks before departure. This includes vaccinations and understanding the health risks of your specific destination (like heat, humidity, or altitude in the Himalayas).
  • Responsible Conduct:Travelers should be mindful of local laws and customs. Travel isn't just about seeing a place; it's about respecting the "sanitation, hygiene standards, and safety" of the local community.
  • The "During & After" Rule:Stay mindful of food and water safety. If you feel unwell after returning, the WHO emphasizes informing doctors of your full travel history—this is key to global health safety.
